Richard Ugino is a professional actor and singer based in New York City.

His work has been seen on stage nationally in venues including the legendary Apollo Theater in New York. He has collaborated with Tony Award-winning and Grammy-nominated artists on new theatrical projects as well as established pieces. He has maintained an active career across a variety of entertainment media.

Richard made his professional stage debut in Michael Donald Edwards’ production of Amadeus at Syracuse Stage. Shortly thereafter, he was cast in the first national touring company of If This Hat Could Talk—a civil rights movement musical directed and choreographed by Tony winner George Faison (The Wiz.) Following this tour, Richard continued his work with Faison on a new production of West Side Story, performed at the Baltimore Lyric Opera House.

He has appeared on the New York stage in productions of Obama 44 (La MaMa e.t.c.), Macbeth and The Taming of the Shrew (CSV Cultural Center), Tartuffe (Theatre Row) and The Caucasian Chalk Circle (45 Bleecker). In addition, he has performed at Birdland Jazz Club, and regularly appears in readings and workshops in New York of new material, both musical and legit. As part of the RIPFEST filmmaking project, Richard performed in “The One That I Want”, a short film directed by Sean Gannet. The film was subsequently shown at the Staten Island Film Festival.

His extensive voiceover work includes narration, live announce and audiobook collaborations. He has repeatedly lent his voice to recordings by Full Cast Audio, young adult fiction author Bruce Coville’s audiobook publishing company. His Full Cast appearances include James Howe’s The Misfits and Tamora Pierce’s Circle of Magic series. Richard also recently completed narration for the DVD supplement of the textbook Instrumentation for Audiology and Hearing Science from Plural Publishing, Inc.

A graduate of Syracuse University with a BFA in Musical Theatre, Richard performed in numerous productions including Billy Bishop Goes to War—a multi-character solo performance piece with music—which served as his honors thesis from the University. While in the MFA Graduate Acting program at Brooklyn College, Richard appeared in The Thugs (Bart) directed by Welker White, Dancing at Lughnasa (Michael) directed by Mary Beth Easley, and Julius Caesar (Brutus) directed by William Addis.

Richard is a proud member of Actors’ Equity Association, the union of professional actors and stage managers in the United States.
